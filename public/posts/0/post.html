Hello, welcome to my photo blog! I'm not sure what I want to do here, exactly. I'd like to put my photos up so they can be accessed by anyone. I don't like the idea of posting my photos to social media. I don't like the way social media algorithmizes everything and makes my brain seek external validation.

Some photos have a story behind them, pleasant or otherwise. For others, I'd just like to analyze what I like about it or what I think I could have done differently. Maybe some posts won't have text at all.

Anyway, this post is meant to be a few of my favourite photos since I started photography in January 2025.

This was a nice afternoon. I live in the West End neighbourhood of Vancouver, BC, and I find it to be quite chatty. As I was taking pictures, I probably had 4 or 5 people stop and talk to me.

    "Beautiful, aren't they?"

    "Are you getting any good pictures?"

    "It's pretty early for these to be out, but I'm not complaining."

I'm not sure if this would happen in other neighbourhoods of Vancouver. Maybe Commercial Drive. I've only been living downtown since June 2025, so I'm not really the person to ask.

Anyway, I like the bokeh, the blue sky, and the building in the background. I've been trying to take more pictures of cherry blossoms but I find it difficult. The blossoms are small, so it's tempting to get close. Often though, the photo is quite busy with the rest of the tree and blossoms in the background. Heavy bokeh helps, or try to find a branch like this where the background is more empty. I haven't learned how to take a good photo of the entire tree, it just never comes out right.

The next photo illustrates my point about the busyness.

Personally, I like this photo a lot, but people seem to prefer the first one.

I love photographing on an overcast day. It makes it much easier to create compositions such as these where strong colours and lines can stand out against a totally white background. This photo feels a bit otherworldly to me, as it's hard to tell WHERE the drill even is with nothing to compare it against. I have found when people react to a photo as \"striking\" it's usually when there are only a handful of colours in the photo, as here. This is anecdotal and this ill-informed observation will likely change over time.

This one is kind of funny, I feel like it's saying something with the misspelled \"World Peace,\" dead plants, and a broken dish in the background. I tried adding a bit of bokeh, I notice that the first couple of letters are blurred when that's maybe not what I wanted. I can't get very close to objects with my camera, they become out of focus.

The photography class I was enrolled in was delighted by this one. It's fun to take confusing photographs, people spend time looking at it trying to guess what it is. But sometimes I get a bit annoyed with a photograph that seems to only find its value in being confusing. I hope the reflections and colour in the metal are interesting in and of themselves.

While taking photos of this thing, someone asked me if I could take their photo in front of the flame with their film camera. I sheepishly gave a sort of \"okay, well, I'll try...\" which is just the worst kind of response you can possibly give. Just say you're going to do it or don't, don't make people nervous with your insecurity. I think I felt so new to photography still that I didn't feel deserving of their trust. It didn't help that it was a film camera. I generally take a few photos to find the exposure settings I want. We can only hope they had the auto mode on their camera. Given the chance I would have talked to them a bit longer about their camera, because it was interesting, but I felt like I took probably took a bad photo, and I was embarrassed, so I left. It'd be nice to see it.
